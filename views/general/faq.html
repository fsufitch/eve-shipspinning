What is this "ship spinning" thing?

Ship Spinning Inc. is a corporation in the massively multiplayer online role-playing game (MMORPG) called Eve Online. It provides slot machines based on the in-game currency, ISK (Inter-Stellar Kredits) for the enjoyment of the players and profit of the corporation (and with some luck, the player's profit as well).

But why is it called "ship spinning"?

Eve Online has a feature informally dubbed "ship spinning". Whenever a pilot docks his or her ship in a station, a 3rd person view of the ship becomes available, which can be interacted with by clicking, dragging, and scrolling to spin the ship or to zoom in and out. For reference, use the video below:

This much-loved feature was replaced in the Incarna expansion by a 3rd person view of the actual pilot outside his or her ship, which was much less fun to spin.

At the time of its conception, Ship Spinning Inc. sought to alleviate pilots' hurt feelings by providing an alternative way of "spinning" ships: on the reels of a slot machine. It's not the same, I know, but it's something.

Since then, ship spinning was re-introduced to Eve due to the massive player outcry. While this may seem like it makes Ship Spinning Inc. redundant, spinning ships on a slot machine still has an advantage over vintage ship spinning: you can win shiny new ships!

How do I play?

You can find the available spinning stations under the "Spin now!" link at the top of the page. You choose the station, make sure you have your four-leaf clover, rabbit's foot, or whatever lucky charm you prefer, pull the lever, and see what turns out.

Wait, but you mentioned profit. Do I have to pay?

Yes, like any game of chance with real prizes, it does cost something to play. The price of each pull varies from station to station, so make sure to check the different prices out.

How do I pay?

In-game, donate however much ISK you want to donate to Ship Spinning Inc. Due to lag because of caching in the API, it may take up to 30 minutes for it to be credited to your account. Feel free to test the waters with 1 or 2 ISK to check that it does indeed show up on the site. Please take note: Excepting extenuating circumstances, ISK will not be released back to you once donated.

How are prizes delivered?

Because the automatic API can only give read-only access to what's going on in-game, there is no automated process for prize delivery. This means that a Ship Spinning Inc. agent will have to manually contract you your prize. Please allow up to 48 hours for the prize contract to be assigned. Be advised that, in order to accomodate other winners, these contracts are limited time. If you need special accomodations, contact us.

Where are prizes delivered?

Due to limited monetary and transport resources, all prizes are delivered to the Jita 4-4 trade hub for the time being. If you would like to have your prizes transported, Ship Spinning Inc. heartily recommends Red Frog Freight (and their sister services) for safe and fast transport.

Wait... How can I trust you?

"Send me your ISK and I will send you 2x the amount back!" Yeah, you probably hear that a lot if you hang around trade hubs. There have even been some crazy money-grabbing schemes in Eve Online's history. However, Ship Spinning Inc. is not in the scamming business.

I could list you a bunch of reasons why a slot machine game is not a viable scamming scheme, but you could just take a look at the list of winners and ask them yourself.

Okay, but how do I know the slots aren't rigged? Couldn't someone review your code?

Before I launched Ship Spinning Inc., I contacted Chribba for a code review. He argued that while he could definitely do so, I could also immediately revert to a rigged version of the code, rendering his review moot. I could even disguise this as a new feature release, or a bug fix. Requiring him, or anyone else, to continually review every change to the codebase would be unfair to them and their spare time.

tl;dr: You'll just have to trust me on this, at least until you play and win.

Do you offer some sort of statistics on the payout of each station?

Yes, in fact! We offer up-to-date payout readings for every single station. The system will also automatically disable reels that fail to live up to our 90% payout promise. We also plan to offer more statistics, once time allows their implementation.

Does [SLOTS] engage in RMT, money laundering, or other shady activities?

Absolutely not. Ship Spinning Inc. has the utmost respect for Eve, the Eve community, and its rules. This is a completely legal operation involving only in-game items and ISK. There is no special treatment based on any out-of-game behavior, and there is zero tolerance of anything breaking the Eve EULA or Terms of Service.
